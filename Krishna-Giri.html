About Dashain Festival

Dashain, also known as Vijaya Dashami, is the greatest and most widely celebrated Hindu festival in Nepal. It typically falls in September or October (depending on the lunar Bikram Sambat calendar) and lasts 15 days. Dashain is much more than a festival.

It brings Nepalis in Nepal and abroad together and provides a sense of unity. For this reason, the importance of Dashain has gone up at a time when disunity and fractured relations have become much more common. In addition to Nepal, Hindus in India, Sri Lanka, and Bangladesh celebrate Dashain.

About ID Festival

The festival of Eid al-Fitr, which marks the end of the Muslim holy month of Ramadan, is celebrated all over the world. Muslims observe Ramadan by reading the Qur’an, emphasizing charity (zakat), abstaining from food and drink during daylight hours, and concentrating on prayer and study to increase their taqwa, or sacred consciousness. Observation of Ramadan is one of the five pillars of Islam.

Eid al-Fitr, often shortened to “Eid,” is a joyous celebration traditionally lasting three days. Muslims mark the occasion with private and community events such as communal prayer (salat), parties, preparing and eating special foods, giving gifts, wearing new clothes, and visiting friends and family. A few days before Eid al-Fitr, Muslims make donations of food to the poor—sadaqah al-fitr (charity of fast-breaking)—so that all may mark the end of fasting with a special meal for the holiday.

About Tihar and Chhat Festival

Tihar and Chhat are two vibrant festivals celebrated in Nepal and some parts of India, steeped in tradition and cultural significance. Tihar, also known as Deepawali, is a five-day festival of lights that falls in late October or early November. Chhat, on the other hand, is a four-day festival dedicated to the Sun god Surya and Chhathi Maiyya, the goddess associated with fasting and purity, observed around six days after Tihar.

Here's a glimpse into the unique traditions of each festival:

Tihar

  • Day 1 (Kag Tihar): This day honors crows, believed to be messengers of Yama, the god of death. Offerings of sweets and grains are made to appease them.
  • Day 2 (Kukur Tihar): The second day is dedicated to dogs, considered loyal companions and protectors. They are adorned with marigolds and offered delicious food.
  • Day 3 (Gai Tihar): Cows are worshipped on this day as a symbol of prosperity and motherhood. They are decorated with colorful fabrics and offered puja (prayers).
  • Day 4 (Govardhan Puja & Diwali): The fourth day coincides with Diwali, the festival of lights. Homes are illuminated with diyas (oil lamps), symbolizing the victory of good over evil. Lakshmi, the goddess of wealth, is also worshipped on this day.
  • Day 5 (Bhai Tika): The final day celebrates the bond between siblings. Sisters apply tikas (colorful paste) on their brothers' foreheads, wishing them well-being and prosperity.

Chhat

  • Day 1 (Nahay Khaay): On the first day, devotees take a holy bath in a river or pond and observe a strict vegetarian diet.
  • Day 2 ( Kharna): The second day involves preparing prasad (ritualistic food offerings) of thekua (deep-fried wheat flour dough) and kheer (rice pudding).
  • Day 3 (Sandhya Arghya): This is the most significant day where devotees offer prayers and offerings to the setting sun at a riverbank or any natural water source.
  • Day 4 (Usha Arghya): Chhat concludes with offerings to the rising sun, symbolizing gratitude and the completion of the rituals.

Both Tihar and Chhat are vibrant celebrations that strengthen social bonds and express reverence for nature and the divine.

The Beautiful Place of Nepal

Nepal, a landlocked country nestled in South Asia, boasts a captivating beauty that transcends description. From the snow-capped peaks of the Himalayas to the lush valleys and serene lakes, Nepal offers a diverse landscape that caters to every traveler's interest.

Here are some of the most beautiful places in Nepal:

  • Pokhara: This enchanting city nestled beside Phewa Lake is a haven for adventure seekers and nature lovers alike. Glide on a colorful boat across the Phewa Lake, or trek to the vantage point of Sarangkot for a breathtaking panorama of the Annapurna range.
  • Everest Base Camp: Embark on a challenging yet rewarding trek to Everest Base Camp, where you'll stand amidst the world's tallest mountains. Witness the majestic Everest towering above you, and experience the thrill of being surrounded by such an awe-inspiring natural wonder.
  • Lumbini: Lumbini, the birthplace of Lord Buddha, is a sacred site for Buddhists around the world. Immerse yourself in the spiritual ambiance as you explore the Maya Devi Temple, where Buddha was born, and the serene monastic gardens.
  • Chitwan National Park: This UNESCO World Heritage Site is a haven for wildlife enthusiasts. Explore the park on elephant back or in a jeep safari de, and be on the lookout for one-horned rhinos, majestic Bengal tigers, and a variety of bird species.
